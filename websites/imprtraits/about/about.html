Process

  • Gathering reference images: The artist typically starts by obtaining high-quality images of the pet from the client. These images serve as reference for the artist in creating a likeness of the pet in the portrait.

  • Sketching: Next, the artist uses digital software to create a rough sketch of the pet. This step involves blocking out the basic shapes and forms of the pet's face and body.

  • Refining the sketch: In this stage, the artist adds more details to the sketch, such as the pet's fur, eyes, and other features. The artist also adds shading to give the portrait a more lifelike appearance.

  • Coloring: Once the sketch is complete, the artist adds color to the portrait. This involves selecting appropriate colors for the pet's fur, eyes, and other features, and applying them to the portrait.

  • Final touches: Finally, the artist makes any final adjustments to the portrait, such as fine-tuning the lighting, adding texture to the fur, and making any other changes to improve the overall appearance of the portrait.

  • Delivery: After the portrait is complete, the artist delivers the final digital file to the client, who can then use it however they wish, such as printing it as a wall art or using it as a digital display.
